Kanji Morphing

- 漢字モーフィング -

Demo Video
Description

Input text is decomposed into individual kanji characters. Adjacent pairs are morphed using a flow-matching diffusion model and played as a continuous loop.

Noise is intentionally injected at segment midpoints, making visible the process by which the model extracts kanji forms from chaos. At 2 steps, forms are turbulent and ambiguous; at 160 steps, sharp black-and-white characters emerge.

In the three-screen projection installation, sequential symbol flows are projected onto left, center, and right walls — immersing the viewer in a field of transforming signs.
